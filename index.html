MoretonJazz

Welcome to 'Moreton Jazz’! At our monthly concerts, you’ll hear nothing but entertaining jazz music with melody, great harmony and a true sense of swing. Established and run by Ben Holder and Paul Jefferies, the club has already earned a reputation for having a friendly, welcoming candle-lit atmosphere - call or email us to book a table, we’re confident you’ll like what you see and hear.

What’s On

Rebecca Poole Quartet (feat. guitarist Hugh Turner)

February 8th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Sassy, sultry, engaging and thoroughly gorgeous music from this London based songstress with surprising musical twists throughout.

Read MORE Book

Rebecca is one heck of a singer. She draws upon music from the likes of Ella, O’Day and Simone but references some more contemporary greats like Amy Winehouse and Melody Gardot. However, this is no mere ‘replicator' - Rebecca very much has her own style (she’s a fine composer too) so it’s natural that she’s been chosen by the likes of Jools Holland to support when he’s in town and to collaborate with Jamiroqaui’s Matt Johnson. Performing with Rebecca will be one of the country’s finest guitarists - Hugh Turner - whose blend of acoustic guitar jazz and funky groove is mesmerising in its own right. Ben Holder plays violin and Paul Jefferies will root proceedings on double bass and misery stick!

Tom Kincaid Trio

March 8th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Tom returns to Moreton Jazz to wow us with more of his pianistic wizardry, but this time it’s the music of Cole Porter that he’ll be presenting.

Read MORE Book

Tom is regarded by those who know as one Britain’s finest traditional and stride pianists. Think Teddy Wilson or Art Tatum, then add a contemporary harmonic twist or two and you’ve got the picture. His lightness of touch belies the power that lurks beneath a fearsome jazz technique and that’s what makes this great musician so dynamic and listenable. Tom tells us he’s chosen the music of Cole Porter because of its lyricism - and the wealth of melodies you can find in the popular composer’s lexicon. He’s right of course with tunes like It’s De-lovely, Night & Day, Love for Sale and I Get A Kick Out Of You - you know you’re in for a musical treat. Ben Holder will be adding lyrics and some nifty violin and Paul Jefferies has the bass end covered.

Michael Roberts Trio

April 12th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Full throttle, smooth and soulful - award winning singer Michael will have your feet tapping from the very first note.

Read MORE Book

We’re proud to present one of the UK’s very finest male jazz singers. Michael will be bringing you the music of the late, great USA legend Mark Murphy. With Mark Rose on guitar, Ben Holder on violin and Paul Jefferies on bass, this promises to be a corker of a gig with a wealth of styles and music. This will be jazz with oodles of swing, Latin grooves, fabulous melodies and tunes like Baubles, Bangles & Beads, Stolen Moments and Cantaloupe Island. Mark Murphy was the archetypal jazz singer: the term ‘Vocaleese’was almost invented for him. His clear diction, pure tone and jazzy delivery earmarked Murphy as a singer of rare talent and one of post Hard Bop’s true singing legends.

Kourosh Kanani Trio

May 10th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

One of the new breed of British jazz ‘Manouche’ guitarists with a fiery technique, superb sense of swing and a staggering knowledge of the Django catalogue!

Read MORE Book

We like to do our bit for the Gypsy Swing Jazz movement - after all it’s where Ben kicked off his career, so we couldn’t be more pleased to introduce this exciting new young guitarist. Kourosh has spent the last two year away from his native Leeds, in London, where he’s making surfboard sized waves! Seen as one of the players to watch out for and to play with, he follows in the steps of greats like Martin Taylor, Remi Harris and Gary Potter. Most gypsy swing players in the UK come out of the rock or heavy metal school of learning, but Khourosh has studied jazz from the get-go and it shows. Rob Terry Trio + Ben Holder

June 14th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

Gracefully combining classical masterpieces with jazz standards and introducing sparkling new originals.

Read MORE Book

One of our favourite performances of 2016 - Rob Terry Trio’s music is genre busting! Take a large helping of western classical music and thread through a seam of jazz improvisation, add some elements of swing and a sprinkling of prog-rock and you’re getting close to describing the sound these guys make. It rivets the listener with its flashes of virtuosity, with moments of ‘I know that piece’ and it can lull you into a nirvana of ballad like harmony but it always remains fresh and exciting. Pianist Rob created this mix of ideas and styles whilst studying music and composition at Birmingham Conservatoire and since then he’s worked with Eartha Kit, Gloria Gaynor, Dave Stuart (Eurythmics) and Lulu. He’s worked with a who’s who of jazz in Britain but until only a few years ago he had never created a trio of his own to showcase his own music. The trio has invited Ben Holder to play violin with them - expect fireworks and beautiful music.

Bateman Brothers Quartet

July 12th 2017

£12.00 (£10.00 if ordered ahead)

Doors open at 19:15, show starts at 20:00

A trumpet, a trombone, a piano and a double bass - not quite an ‘All Stars’ band line-up, but a recipe for some of the most entertaining traditional jazz you’ll hear in a long while with a distinctly ‘Satchmo’ flavour.

Read MORE Book

Ian and Alan Bateman have worked together for decades, but their true partnership began when they joined forces in 2011 to create a special big band that celebrates the music of Louis Armstrong. Ian has been at the very top of the British jazz scene since the 80s, paying his dues with Acker Bilk, Terry Lightfoot and the Pete Allen bands. His brother Alan has a tone and style that is quite probably the closest you’ll hear to the legendary Luois Armstrong - together they are formidable. Creating a fitting rhythm section, Ben Holder will take to the ‘ivories’ tonight and Paul Jefferies is on bass duty - if this doesn’t swing like a pendulum we’ll eat our hats!
